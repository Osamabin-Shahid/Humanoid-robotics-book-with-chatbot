# Data Model: Module 1 — The Robotic Nervous System (ROS 2)

## Educational Content Structure

### Module Entity
- **Name**: Module 1 - The Robotic Nervous System (ROS 2)
- **Description**: Educational module introducing ROS 2 as the nervous system of humanoid robots
- **Target Audience**: Software developers with basic Python knowledge, AI learners, robotics beginners
- **Chapters**: 3 structured chapters with progressive depth
- **Format**: Docusaurus Markdown documentation

### Chapter Entity
- **Chapter ID**: Sequential identifier (1-3)
- **Title**: Descriptive title matching learning objectives
- **Content**: Markdown-based educational content
- **Learning Objectives**: Specific skills/knowledge to be acquired
- **Prerequisites**: Required knowledge before reading
- **Dependencies**: Other chapters or external resources needed

### Chapter 1: ROS 2 as the Robotic Nervous System
- **Focus**: Conceptual Foundations
- **Topics**:
  - What ROS 2 is and why it exists
  - ROS 2 as middleware for physical AI systems
  - Comparison: Digital AI pipelines vs physical robot control
- **Learning Outcomes**:
  - Explain the purpose and benefits of ROS 2 in robotic systems
  - Articulate the differences between digital AI and physical robot control

### Chapter 2: ROS 2 Middleware for Physical AI
- **Focus**: Connecting AI Agents to Physical Systems
- **Topics**:
  - How ROS 2 facilitates communication between AI and robots
  - Node architecture and communication patterns
  - Message passing and service calls
- **Learning Outcomes**:
  - Identify how ROS 2 facilitates communication between AI agents and physical robots
  - Describe the role of ROS 2 as the communication layer

### Chapter 3: Digital vs Physical AI Systems
- **Focus**: Conceptual Framework for Embodied AI
- **Topics**:
  - Key architectural differences between digital and physical systems
  - Real-time constraints in physical systems
  - Safety and reliability considerations
- **Learning Outcomes**:
  - Distinguish between digital AI and physical robot control systems
  - Apply appropriate ROS 2 patterns instead of pure digital AI approaches

## Content Validation Rules
- All content must be accessible to target audience without requiring prior ROS experience
- Technical concepts must be explained with clear analogies and examples
- Content must follow progressive complexity from basic to advanced concepts
- All information must be source-grounded (based on official ROS 2 documentation)

## State Transitions
- Draft → Review → Approved → Published (for content workflow)
- Conceptual → Practical → Applied (for learning progression)
